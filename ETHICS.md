# Ethics

## Our Ethical Framework

QR-SHIELD is grounded in a commitment to ethical security research and defensive capability. This document outlines the ethical principles that guide the project and expectations for users.

## Core Principles

### 1. Defense First

QR-SHIELD exists to advance defensive security. Every capability in this tool should be understood in the context of defensive evaluation and responsible research.

- **Research is for defense:** Understand offensive techniques to build better defenses

- **Knowledge sharing:** Advance the security community's collective capability

- **Transparency:** Be clear about what the tool does and its implications

- **Responsibility:** Recognize the power and risk of security research tools

### 2. Respect for Privacy and Autonomy

Users of QR-SHIELD must respect the privacy, security, and autonomy of others.

- **Consent is fundamental:** Only test systems you own or have explicit authorization to test

- **Respect boundaries:** Do not exceed the scope of authorized testing

- **Protect data:** Treat captured credentials and session data with extreme care

- **Transparency:** Inform relevant parties before testing

- **Confidentiality:** Protect information discovered during testing

### 3. Legal and Regulatory Compliance

Security research operates within legal frameworks. Users must comply with all applicable laws and regulations.

- **Know the law:** Understand laws in your jurisdiction

- **Stay authorized:** Never conduct unauthorized access

- **Report responsibly:** Disclose findings through proper channels

- **No exploitation:** Do not use research capabilities for harm

- **Professional standards:** Meet recognized industry standards

### 4. Harm Reduction

QR-SHIELD recognizes that security research tools can be misused if deployed outside authorized contexts. We commit to harm reduction:

- **Awareness:** Users understand the risks and implications

- **Deterrence:** Clear statements against misuse

- **Barriers:** Design choices that reduce misuse likelihood

- **Reporting:** Mechanisms to report misuse

- **Accountability:** Consequences for harmful use

### 5. Community and Collaboration

Security advances through collaboration and shared knowledge.

- **Community:** Support the broader security research community

- **Transparency:** Share findings and techniques

- **Mentorship:** Help newer researchers understand principles

- **Diversity:** Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds

- **Inclusivity:** Create space for underrepresented voices

## Ethical Guidelines for Users

### Do

✓ **Conduct authorized security research** - Test only systems you own or have permission to test

✓ **Document everything** - Keep detailed records of testing activities

✓ **Report findings responsibly** - Disclose vulnerabilities through proper channels

✓ **Protect captured data** - Treat sessions and credentials securely

✓ **Support the community** - Share knowledge and findings

✓ **Consider impact** - Think about who might be affected by your research

✓ **Continue learning** - Stay current on security principles and practices

✓ **Seek ethics guidance** - Consult with colleagues or institutional review boards

### Don't

✗ **Conduct unauthorized access** - Never access systems without permission

✗ **Obtain credentials without authorization** - Never use QR-SHIELD to collect or use credentials without permission

✗ **Support deceptive credential collection** - Never use QR-SHIELD for phishing or other deceptive schemes

✗ **Enable fraud** - Never use for financial crimes or fraud

✗ **Harm individuals** - Never conduct testing that harms people

✗ **Violate privacy** - Never capture or use personal data improperly

✗ **Exceed authorization** - Never go beyond agreed testing scope

✗ **Publicize exploits** - Never publicly disclose unpatched vulnerabilities

✗ **Sell or share access** - Never sell captured sessions or credentials

✗ **Cover up misuse** - Never conceal illegal or unethical activity

## Conflicts of Interest

### Recognize Conflicts

Be aware of situations where your interests might conflict with ethics:

- Personal financial gain

- Career advancement

- Competition or rivalry

- Pressure from management

- Time pressure or urgency

- Frustration with slow processes

### Resolve Conflicts

When facing conflicts:

1. **Recognize the conflict** - Acknowledge when interests diverge

1. **Consider ethics first** - Make decisions based on principles

1. **Seek guidance** - Consult colleagues or mentors

1. **Document decisions** - Record your reasoning

1. **Accept consequences** - Be willing to face results of ethical choices

## Responsible Disclosure

When you discover vulnerabilities or security issues:

1. **Report privately** - Contact vendors/platforms first

1. **Allow time** - Give reasonable time for fixes

1. **Avoid publicity** - Don't disclose unpatched vulnerabilities

1. **Coordinate timing** - Work with vendors on disclosure date

1. **Credit researchers** - Acknowledge security researchers

See [RESPONSIBLE_DISCLOSURE.md](RESPONSIBLE_DISCLOSURE.md) for detailed process.

## Decision-Making Framework

When facing ethical questions:

### Ask Yourself

1. **Is it legal?** - Does it comply with applicable laws?

1. **Is it authorized?** - Do I have explicit permission?

1. **Would I be comfortable publicly admitting this?** - Would you explain this to a regulator?

1. **Who might be harmed?** - Who could be negatively affected?

1. **What would a professional security person do?** - Would peers approve?

1. **Would this advance defense or enable attacks?** - Is the benefit defensive?

If you can't answer "yes" to most of these, reconsider.

### Seek Guidance

- Consult colleagues

- Review professional codes

- Contact institutional review boards

- Discuss with mentors

- Document your reasoning
